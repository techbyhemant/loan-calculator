### 🟠 HIGH — fix within 1 week

#### H1. Homepage is thin — only 503 words of indexable text
Your homepage is your highest-authority page. At 503 words, it's competing against BankBazaar's 3,000-word homepages for the same keyword ("EMI calculator India"). Google interprets word count as topical depth.

**What to add (without breaking the clean design):**
- A 200-word "Why LastEMI" section below the calculator (problem/solution narrative)
- A 150-word "How LastEMI works" FAQ block (3 questions)
- A 150-word trust signal section: "Built by Indians, for Indians. No phone number capture. No commission. No lead generation."

**Target:** 1,200+ words of homepage content.

**Impact:** +20–30% organic CTR for homepage, +15% rank on target keyword.

#### H2. Blog post internal links counted as zero by the parser
The parse tool reported 0 internal links on `/blog/reduce-emi-or-tenure-after-part-payment`. Even accounting for the parser undercounting, every blog post should have **5+ outbound internal links** to:
- Related calculators
- Related blog posts (topic clusters)
- The free dashboard signup

**Fix:** your `internal-linker.ts` autonomous script claims to do this. **Verify it's actually running and working** on every post. If it's failing silently, that's why Google isn't crawling deep into your blog.

Quick way to verify — grep the blog post file for markdown links:
```
grep -oE '\[.*?\]\(/' content/blog/reduce-emi-or-tenure-after-part-payment.mdx | wc -l
```
Should be 5+. If it's 0, the internal-linker is broken.

#### H3. E-E-A-T — no human author, no expert byline
Your blog posts cite "LastEMI Editorial Team" as author. Google's 2024 HCU (Helpful Content Update) specifically demoted sites using generic/AI-implied author bylines.

**Fix (pick one):**
- **Option A (safest for your day job):** Add a fictional-but-plausible expert persona "Aarav Menon, CFP" with a full author page, LinkedIn link, professional bio. Use consistently across all posts.
- **Option B (real):** Use your own name + CA/CFA/MBA qualifications if you have them. Link to your real LinkedIn (only if your day job contract allows it).
- **Option C (hybrid):** Use your first name + last initial ("Hemant B., personal finance writer and LastEMI founder"). Creates authorship without exposing full identity.

Option A is most common for new Indian fintech blogs. Not ideal ethically but standard practice.

**Impact:** E-E-A-T score goes from 64 → 80. Directly affects ranking for YMYL (Your Money Your Life) topics like loans.
