## When the Rule Changes
While consolidating personal loans can be a smart move for many borrowers, there are situations where it may not be the best option. For example, if you have high-interest credit card debt or other priority expenses, it may be more beneficial to focus on paying those off first. Additionally, if you have a personal loan with a low interest rate, it may not be worth consolidating, especially if the new loan has a higher interest rate or fees. It's essential to carefully evaluate your financial situation and consider all the pros and cons before making a decision.

One common mistake borrowers make is consolidating their loans without considering the potential impact on their credit score. Consolidation can affect your [credit utilization](https://www.cibil.com/resources/articles/credit-utilization-ratio) ratio, which can in turn affect your credit score. It's essential to evaluate the potential impact on your credit score and consider the potential risks and benefits of consolidation. You should also consider the fees associated with consolidation, such as processing fees, and ensure that the new loan terms align with your financial goals.

## The RBI Rule
As of March 2026, the RBI guidelines do not protect personal loan borrowers from prepayment penalties. This means that if you decide to prepay your consolidated loan, you may be charged a penalty, typically ranging from 2-5% of the outstanding balance. It's crucial to check your loan agreement carefully and understand the prepayment terms before making a decision. On the other hand, if you have a floating-rate home loan, the RBI zero prepayment penalty rule applies, allowing you to prepay your loan without incurring any penalties.
<Callout type="tip">Always review your loan agreement and understand the prepayment terms before making a decision.</Callout>

It's also essential to note that the RBI guidelines do not regulate personal loan interest rates, which means that lenders can charge higher interest rates if they deem it necessary. This can make it challenging for borrowers to compare loan offers and make informed decisions.
